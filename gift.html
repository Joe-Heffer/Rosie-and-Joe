A Little History

A Wedding Gift of Our Own

Eyam Hall has a wedding in its own history. The gritstone Jacobean manor was built in 1671 by Thomas Wright as a wedding present for his son John and his new wife, Elizabeth — and it's been a Wright family home ever since, passed down through nine generations for over 350 years. The family still live there today, alongside the craft centre and gardens open to visitors. We love that the place we're getting married began, more than three centuries ago, as someone else's wedding gift.
